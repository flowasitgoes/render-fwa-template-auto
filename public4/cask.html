/ Introduction

We’re making it possible for everyone to trade bourbon barrels & scotch whisky casks from leading distilleries.

The global market for barreled spirits is booming, with distilleries and brands collaborating to release innovative new products. Young distilleries buy spirits from established ones to launch products faster, while established distilleries and brands seek new offerings to satisfy demand and provide variety. However, as the number of entities and demand rise, finding an easy way to facilitate these transactions has become challenging.

Cask Exchange is an online trading platform where qualified distilleries, wholesalers, and brands can buy and sell barreled spirits for bottling. It connects sellers looking to monetize their barrels with buyers seeking liquid for bottling.

Bringing A Modern Approach
To Barreled Spirit Trading
01
Account Creation

Qualified distilleries, wholesalers and holding companies create an account. These entities are approved for selling and/or buying access based on the documentation they provide. Sellers must have a wholesale license, and buyers must have a DSP number.

02
Cask Listing

Sellers list casks for sale within the marketplace. Ownership documentation is required and verified by Cask Exchange prior to listings going live. Sellers specify an “Ask” prices defining the amount which they would currently accept for the casks

03
Cask Bidding

Approved buyers browse casks within the marketplace. When they identify casks which they would like to acquire they can either accept the current “Ask” price, or they can submit a “Bid” price of their own. If the “Bid” is lower than the current ask then the selling party has the opportunity to adjust their price to the “Bid” if they so choose.

04
Transaction Process

Once there is parity between a “Bid” and “Ask” an exchange is initiated between the two parties. Funds are collected from the buyer and placed into Escrow. The seller provides documentation and any contingent deliverables to the buyer. Once buyer is fully satisfied with the transaction the funds are released to the Seller.
